S:
Patient reports tick bite. Reports a tick bite with a bullseye rash present. No systemic symptoms reported. Exposure occurred in Georgia.
O: Unable to perform physical exam due to phone-only encounter. Patient description consistent with erythema migrans rash.

A:
  - Working diagnosis (most likely): Early localized Lyme disease, suggested by bullseye rash and tick exposure >36 hours in an endemic area.
  - Differential:
    1. Early Lyme disease
    2. Tick-bite hypersensitivity reaction
    3. Southern tick-associated rash illness (STARI)
    4. Cellulitis or other local skin infection
  - Red flags considered: No systemic symptoms such as fever, neck stiffness, neurological signs, or cardiorespiratory symptoms reported.

P:
  - Disposition: Urgent in-person evaluation recommended for clinical confirmation and initiation of treatment.
  - Plan: Advise patient to seek prompt in-person care for physical examination, possible serologic testing, and initiation of antibiotic therapy as per CDC guidelines for early Lyme disease.
  - Safety net: Instruct patient to seek emergency care if fever, worsening rash, joint pain, neurological symptoms, or cardiorespiratory symptoms develop.
