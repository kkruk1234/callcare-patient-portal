a
Only the nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory
drugs (NSAIDs) listed here have FDA approval for use as simple analgesics, but
clinical experience has also been gained with other drugs.
b
Acetaminophen and NSAID dosages for adults weighing less than 50 kg 
should be adjusted for weight.

c
Acetaminophen lacks the peripheral anti-inflammatory and antiplatelet
activities of the other NSAIDs.

d
The standard against which other NSAIDs are compared. May inhibit platelet
aggregation for longer than 1 week and may cause bleeding.
Aspirin is not recommended for pain in children.
e
May have minimal antiplatelet activity.

f
Administration with antacids may decrease absorption.

g
Use limited to 5 days or fewer.

h
Coombs-positive autoimmune hemolytic anemia has been associated with
prolonged use.

i
Has the same gastrointestinal toxic effects as oral NSAIDs.
